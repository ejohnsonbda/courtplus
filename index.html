THE STADIUM

W.E.R. Joell Tennis Stadium

A court with a conscience — Bermuda's national tennis facility and a living monument to the island's civil rights history.

Located just outside Hamilton, this government-run facility offers 8 courts — 3 lit for night play — at just $10/hr. The stadium was renamed in 2003 in honour of William E.R. Joell, who led the fight to desegregate Bermuda's public tennis courts in 1954.

Getting Here: Bus routes 10 & 11 • 10-min walk from Hamilton • Free scooter parking

OUR STORY

From Segregation to Celebration

The history of W.E.R. Joell Tennis Stadium is the history of Bermuda's fight for equality.

Pre-1950s

A Whites-Only Facility

The Government Tennis Stadium operated under a whites-only policy. Black Bermudian tennis players were barred from using the courts, despite paying the taxes that supported them.

Early 1950s

The Match That Changed Everything

American tennis star Althea Gibson visited Bermuda. She played a friendly match with William E.R. Joell and other Black Bermudian players. The stadium manager ordered them to leave. That moment of injustice sparked Joell into action.

1954

A Petition for Justice

Joell asked Russell Dismont — who would later found the PLP — to draft a petition to Parliament. It passed unanimously. The stadium became one of the first public facilities in Bermuda to open to all races.

2003 — Today

Honouring a Hero

In 2003, the government renamed the stadium in honour of William E.R. Joell. Today it welcomes everyone — a sports facility and a living monument to Bermuda's civil rights history.

"We invite you to play, learn, and reflect on how far we have come."
